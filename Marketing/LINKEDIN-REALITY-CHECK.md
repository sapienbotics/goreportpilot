### Q5: What is the realistic reach of a post today given the current connection base?

**Honest estimate: 100–300 views, mostly wrong audience.**

LinkedIn's algorithm works like this:
1. When you post, it shows your content to ~5-10% of your connections first (~53–105 people)
2. It measures early engagement (likes, comments, shares) to decide whether to amplify
3. If those first 50-100 people engage, it pushes to more

With only ~35 relevant connections (3.4% of network), the first wave of viewers will be dominated by Indian startup founders and AI/tech people. They have no emotional resonance with "I spent 6 hours on a client report." They will scroll past.

**Result:** Low early engagement → algorithm doesn't amplify → post dies at 100-300 views.

Compare this to a marketer with 2,000 connections who are 70% marketing professionals — the same post would realistically reach 2,000–10,000+ views because the first-wave engagement comes from the right people who share and comment.

**Realistic first-post impressions today: 150–300.**
**Realistic first-post impressions from relevant ICP: 15–30.**
**Realistic trial signups from first post: 0–1.**

This is NOT a reason to not post — you have to start somewhere. But it sets honest expectations and explains why the first 2–4 weeks will feel like shouting into a void. It takes 30–60 days of consistent posting AND sending 15-20 targeted connection requests daily to build the right audience.
